What Are Deepfakes?

Imagine you're watching a video where your favorite celebrity seems to say something totally strange, like, "I love bananas!" But then you find out that celebrity never said that. Instead, someone used technology to make the celebrity's face and voice look like they were saying those words. This is what we call a deepfake. Deepfakes are videos or pictures that use computer programs to change someone's face or voice to make them say or do things they never actually did. These fakes can look and sound real, but they aren't.

Why Are Deepfakes a Big Problem?

Deepfakes can be funny, but they can also be dangerous. Sometimes, people use deepfakes to trick others into believing false information. This can cause problems for individuals, spread fake news, and even harm someone's reputation.

  • A deepfake could make it look like a famous person said something they never did, causing confusion.
  • People might use deepfakes to trick others into believing fake news or harmful rumors.
  • Deepfakes can damage reputations or cause misunderstandings.
