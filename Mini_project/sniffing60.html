Online Sniffing Threats for Seniors (60+)

What Is Online Sniffing?

The internet is an amazing place, but just like in the real world, there are risks. One of the online dangers is something called online sniffing, which is when bad actors (hackers) secretly monitor what you're doing online to steal personal information like passwords, bank details, or sensitive messages.

Think of online sniffing as someone secretly listening in on your phone call or watching your mail as it’s being delivered. Hackers use sniffing to "eavesdrop" on your internet activity to steal valuable information.

  • Passwords for your email, social media, or banking accounts.
  • Credit card numbers when you shop online.
  • Personal messages or data you send on the internet.

Why Should You Care?

Hackers often target older adults because they may:

  • Be less familiar with online security tools.
  • Use less secure devices or public Wi-Fi networks.

If your information is stolen, it can lead to:

  • Financial loss: Money stolen from your bank accounts.
  • Identity theft: Hackers pretending to be you to open accounts or take loans.
  • Loss of privacy: Your private communications could be exposed.

How Does Online Sniffing Happen?

  • Unsecured Websites: Hackers watch data sent over insecure websites (websites that don’t start with "https://").
  • Public Wi-Fi Networks: Wi-Fi in coffee shops, libraries, or hotels can be easily hacked.
  • Malware (Bad Software): Hackers trick you into installing harmful software on your computer or phone that monitors your activity.
  • Fake Websites: These websites look real but are set up by hackers to steal your information.

Simple Tips to Stay Safe Online

  • Only Use Secure Websites: Look for a lock icon in your browser bar or "https://" in the website address before entering personal information.
  • Avoid Public Wi-Fi: Don’t connect to Wi-Fi in places like airports, cafes, or hotels unless absolutely necessary. If you must, avoid entering sensitive information.
  • Use Strong Passwords: Create passwords that are hard to guess (e.g., include a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols).
  • Keep Your Software Updated: Update your phone, computer, and antivirus software regularly.
  • Be Wary of Suspicious Emails: Don’t reply or click on links in emails asking for personal information.
  • Install Antivirus Software: Use trusted antivirus programs to protect your device.
  • Use a VPN: A VPN encrypts your online activity and keeps it private.

How to Spot Online Sniffing Threats

  • Strange Pop-Ups or Ads: These could indicate malware is installed on your device.
  • Slow Internet Speeds: This may mean someone is intercepting your data.
  • Unusual Account Activity: Check your bank or email accounts for suspicious activity.
  • Frequent Logouts: Being logged out of your accounts unexpectedly could be a warning sign.

What to Do If You Suspect a Problem

  • Disconnect From the Internet: Turn off Wi-Fi or unplug your internet connection.
  • Run an Antivirus Scan: Use your antivirus software to check for malware.
  • Change Your Passwords: Update passwords for sensitive accounts like email or banking.
  • Seek Help: If you're unsure what to do, ask a trusted person for assistance.

Stay informed and take steps to protect yourself from online sniffing threats!
