What Is Online Sniffing?

The internet is an amazing place to explore, learn, and connect with others. This report will help you understand online sniffing, recognize its dangers, and learn how to stay safe.

Online sniffing is a type of cyber spying where hackers or bad actors use special tools to "sniff" or track data being transmitted over the internet. Think of it like someone eavesdropping on a private conversation.

What hackers steal: Passwords, personal information, photos and videos, or bank details (if shared devices are used).

Why Are Children at Risk?

Children and teenagers often use the internet for:

  • Social media
  • Online gaming
  • Streaming videos
  • Chatting with friends
  • School projects

Hackers target children because they might:

  • Use weak passwords or share information openly.
  • Not recognize phishing links or fake websites.
  • Use public Wi-Fi, making their devices vulnerable.

Types of Sniffing Threats You Should Know

  • Public Wi-Fi Sniffing: Hackers steal data from unsecured Wi-Fi networks.
  • Phishing Links: Fake emails or messages trick you into giving away personal details.
  • Fake Apps or Games: Some free apps contain malware that spies on your device.
  • Social Engineering: Hackers pretend to be someone you trust to steal information.
  • Malware-infected Devices: Malware collects your data silently.

How You Should Stay Safe

  • Protect Your Accounts: Use strong passwords with letters, numbers, and special characters. Never share your passwords. Enable two-factor authentication (2FA).
  • Be Careful on Public Wi-Fi: Avoid sensitive activities on public Wi-Fi. Use a VPN for secure browsing.

  • Install Apps Safely: Download apps only from official app stores. Read reviews before downloading.
  • Keep Devices Updated: Install updates for apps and operating systems.
  • Avoid Oversharing Online: Don‚Äôt share personal details like your name, address, or phone number. Keep profiles private.
  • Use Antivirus Software: Install and regularly update antivirus software. Scan your device for malware.
