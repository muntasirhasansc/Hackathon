For Ages 18-50: Recognizing Phishing Scams

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a cyber scam where criminals trick you into sharing sensitive information like passwords, bank details, or Social Security numbers. They often pretend to be legitimate organizations or people you trust.

How Do Phishing Scams Work?

  • Phishing Emails: Fake emails that ask you to click links or provide details.
  • Vishing (Phishing Calls): Scammers call pretending to be from your bank or a trusted company.
  • Smishing (Phishing Texts): Text messages with fake links or urgent requests.
  • Fake Websites: Websites that mimic real ones to steal your credentials.

How to Spot a Phishing Scam

  • Check email addresses carefully. Are there slight misspellings or extra characters?
  • Be suspicious of urgent language like "Your account will be locked."
  • Look for poor grammar or spelling errors in the message.
  • Hover over links to check if the URL looks legitimate before clicking.

What Should You Do?

  • Don‚Äôt respond or click suspicious links.
  • Verify messages with the official source using their website or phone number.
  • Report phishing attempts to your email provider or cybersecurity agency.
